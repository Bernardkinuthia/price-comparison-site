Product Information

Q: How do you calculate "Price per Watt"?
A: Price per Watt is calculated by dividing the current Amazon price by the generator's maximum output wattage. This metric helps you compare the value of different generators regardless of their size. Lower price per watt generally indicates better value.
Q: What's the difference between battery and gas generators?
A: Battery generators (portable power stations) are quiet, emission-free, and can be used indoors, but have limited runtime and need recharging. Gas generators provide longer runtime and higher power output but produce emissions, are louder, and must be used outdoors.
Q: What does "Wh" mean in battery capacity?
A: "Wh" stands for Watt-hours, which indicates how much energy a battery can store. For example, a 1000Wh battery can theoretically power a 100W device for 10 hours or a 1000W device for 1 hour.
Q: Are refurbished or used generators worth buying?
A: Used generators can offer significant savings, but consider the risks: limited or no warranty, unknown maintenance history, and potential reliability issues. Always check the seller's return policy and Amazon's A-to-Z Guarantee coverage.

Q: Why don't some generators show battery capacity?
A: Traditional gas, diesel, and propane generators don't have batteries - they generate power directly from fuel combustion. Only electric generators (battery-powered portable power stations) and solar generators have battery capacity measurements.
